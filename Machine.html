Machine Learning based on Coin Prediction

In this report, machine learning techniques are employed to predict the success of crowdfunding campaigns, particularly those based on blockchain Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs). The dataset comprises 2767 observations and 16 features, including campaign attributes such as coin price, team size, campaign duration, and online presence indicators.
Data preprocessing involved addressing missing values, handling outliers, and standardizing inconsistent entries. Key features like campaign rating and distributed percentage were highlighted as crucial factors for prediction. Multiple machine learning models were applied, including Decision Tree, Adaptive Boosting, Random Forest, Support Vector Machine (SVM), and K-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN).
Random Forest outperformed other models with an accuracy of 68.25%, sensitivity of 44.12%, and an AUC score of 67.66%. The report emphasizes that factors such as campaign duration, team size, rating, and presence on platforms like GitHub and Reddit significantly influence campaign success. The models provide valuable insights to campaign organizers for optimizing strategies to improve funding outcomes.
